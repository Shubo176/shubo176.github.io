About

I am an undergraduate focused on AI for Science. I build learning systems that integrate sequence, structure, and signal to model biological phenomena and reduce experimental cost. My current work includes (1) detecting DNA modifications from Nanopore current traces with Transformer encoders, and (2) predicting protein melting temperature by fusing ESM sequence embeddings, AlphaFold structural features, and biophysical descriptors with an MLP regressor. I care about rigorous evaluation, hyper-parameter search, and out-of-distribution generalization. Technically I use Python and PyTorch with Transformers, ESM, AlphaFold, Rosetta, Optuna, and common scientific ML tooling. In the future, I plan to continue pursuing research in AI for Science, exploring more scalable and interpretable models that bridge machine learning and molecular biology.
